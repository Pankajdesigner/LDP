More about Conclusive Title

  • What is conclusive title?

    A conclusive title is defined as conclusive proof of ownership of property. In such a system, a title guarantee and insurance are often provided for indemnifying the property holder against any loss arising due to inaccuracies in title. However, in India, land ownership is determined through various documents such as sale deeds, revenue records and survey documents. These documents are not a government guarantee for title but merely a record of the transfer of property making them presumptive in nature.

  • Advantages of conclusive titleship?

    Access to records in single window
    Reduction in litigation
    Tamper-proof titles
    Eliminate fraud
    No duplication or suppression of deeds
    No double registration

  • Any initiative undertaken by the Government?

    The government is keen to push for conclusive land titling and it has released a draft model “Land Title Act” through its policy think tank NITI Ayog, which shall pave the way for conclusive title as against presumptive title system. The intent to move toward conclusive title is further evidenced by the land policy adopted by states such as Maharashtra, Karnataka, Rajasthan etc.

  • Other countries using conclusive title?

    The following are few of the countries that utilise the conclusive titling system : Australia, New Zealand, Most Provinces of Canada, Republic of Ireland, parts of United States of America, Puerto Rico, Philippines, Fiji and Kenya among others.
